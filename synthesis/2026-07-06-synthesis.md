## 2. Which hypotheses moved?

- **H1 — beyond tables/forms:** strengthened. Composability is now valuable primarily as the day-1 rail for Value Loops, not as the strategic moat.
- **H2 — mid-market operations hub:** materially strengthened. Internal VoC shows source-of-truth, inventory, procurement, COGS, retail/EDI, order routing, and failing-ERP pain across 66 substantive discovery records.
- **H3 — focus on resource planning:** strengthened. Internal requirements cluster around operational resource planning and the accounting boundary; HR/CRM-style suite expansion remains unsupported.
- **H4 — value front and center:** strengthened and reframed. The sales value story should be loop activation and outcome receipts, not generic AI or platform flexibility.
- **H5 — dynamic agentic ERP:** refined. The implementation agent should be measured by loop activation at the external boundary, not by generic setup completion.
- **H6 — stable demand / accelerating operations:** moved to gathering evidence. Internal demand for inventory trust, planning, channel/retail readiness, and scaling without headcount supports operational-change pressure, though macro SKU/channel trend data is still needed.
- **H7 — exceptions-first runtime agency:** partially challenged/refined. V3's deduction/compliance wedge has strong economics, but internal value ranking says Close and Retail rails must be trustworthy first; chargebacks/deductions are best treated as a post-Retail loop, not the first hardening target.
- **H8 — moat is the loop:** moved to gathering evidence. LIR provides the metric form of the moat: automatic transactional throughput plus no work-leaving-DOSS events.
