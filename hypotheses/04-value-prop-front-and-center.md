# H4 — Value isn't positioned front and center in sales conversations

**Status:** gathering evidence
**Opened:** 2026-07-06
**Source:** [Kurt's initial observations](../inputs/notes/2026-07-06-initial-observations.md) — from listening to recorded calls

## Claim

Current sales conversations sound like traditional ERP: integration specifics, feature mechanics, down-in-the-weeds detail. The customer pain and the AI-enabled secret sauce are not leading the conversation. Integration detail matters — but it should *close* deals that a sharp value story opened, not carry the whole conversation.

## Why it matters

- If we can't articulate the value crisply, either (a) we haven't found it yet — which is a strategy problem ([H1](01-beyond-tables-and-forms.md)), or (b) we've found it but aren't saying it — which is a positioning/enablement problem. Distinguishing (a) from (b) is urgent.
- Selling on integration specifics is fighting on the incumbent's turf: they will always have more connectors and more reference architectures.

## What "front and center" would look like

A first call where the first 10 minutes are about the customer's operational pain and a before/after picture (e.g., "implementation in weeks," "one live picture of your whole operation," "the system adapts when your business changes") — and integration specifics come up only as proof.

## Evidence for

- Kurt's read of recorded calls to date (2026-07-06): value not leading; conversations resemble traditional ERP sales. *(Link specific call notes in [inputs/calls/](../inputs/calls/) as they're captured.)*

## Evidence against

- 2026-07-06: The *website* leads with value — "speed up every step of your value chain by 10x," customer hours-saved proof points ([doss.com research](../inputs/research/2026-07-06-doss-positioning.md)). The gap appears to be in the sales motion, not the marketing message — i.e., problem (b), enablement, more than problem (a), missing strategy.

## V3 update (2026-07-06): the pitch content now exists

The missing "sharp value story" has two evidence-backed candidates from the proof-points work: **the deduction number** ("machine-speed enforcement deserves machine-speed defense; we recovered/prevented $X in 90 days") and **the Zara-loop narrative** ("the operational edge of Zara/Shein/e.l.f., delivered as product to $20–250M brands"). The e.l.f.-runs-NetSuite footnote is the proof that software alone isn't the pitch — capability is. See [V3 deep dive](../deepdives/2026-07-06-proof-points-v3.md).

## Refinement (2026-07-06)

Two sharper sub-claims worth testing:
1. **The proof economics are the root cause.** Sales can't lead with value because the quantified value is "hours saved" (productivity-tool pricing), not value-chain outcomes (inventory turns, working capital). See STRATEGY.md "proof-economics upgrade."
2. **Dual positioning creates weeds-diving.** If the buyer can't tell whether DOSS is a layer or a replacement, the conversation defaults to integration mechanics — exactly what Kurt heard.

## V4 update (2026-07-06): the value story is an outcome receipt

Additional evidence for:
- 2026-07-06 (V4): Internal LIR work says customers retain when a recurring transaction cycle runs automatically through DOSS and stays there. The value proposition can be stated as loop activation plus an outcome receipt: close in days, compliant retail round-trips, weekly acknowledged POs, daily reconciled inventory, zero manual fallback ([Value Loops / LIR](../inputs/internal/Value-Loops---Outcome-Based-Productization-and-the-DOSS-LIR.html)).
- 2026-07-06 (V4): Buyer language from discovery notes is already outcome-shaped: "run the whole business from one set of numbers," "know exactly what I have," "scale without scaling headcount," "ship retail-compliant on day one," and "keep my GL, lose the ERP pain" ([internal VoC](../inputs/internal/Top-10-Customer-ERP-Ops-Requirements---1H-FY26-SPICED-LIR.html)).

Current synthesis: lead discovery with the buyer's loop and the receipt it will produce; integration detail should prove the activation predicate, not become the pitch.
