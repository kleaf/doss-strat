# H1 — Differentiation must go beyond "tables and forms"

**Status:** gathering evidence
**Opened:** 2026-07-06
**Source:** [Kurt's initial observations](../inputs/notes/2026-07-06-initial-observations.md)

## Claim

A modern UI on the classic ERP shape (tables, forms, reports) is not durable differentiation. If DOSS is "NetSuite but nicer," we lose — incumbents can modernize their face faster than we can build their depth. There must be a structural difference in what the product *is*, not just how it looks.

## Why it matters

This determines everything downstream: positioning, roadmap priorities, and what we demo. It is also the question a sophisticated buyer (or investor) asks first: "what's actually different about this from the last 30 years of ERP?"

## Candidate answers (to be tested)

1. **The system is adaptive, not static** — see [H5](05-agentic-dynamic-erp.md). The differentiation is in the lifecycle (implementation, change, evolution), not the screens.
2. **The unit of value is the value chain, not the module** — see [H2](02-midmarket-operations-hub.md). Incumbents sell modules; we sell one operational picture.
3. **Agentic operation** — the ERP does work (reconciliation, exception handling, replenishment suggestions) rather than only recording work.
4. *(Anti-answer to watch for:* "AI chat over your ERP data" — thin, easily copied, probably not it.)*

## Evidence for

- 2026-07-06: DOSS's own public pitch is literally built on "tables, forms, and workflows" as the three no-code blocks ([doss.com research](../inputs/research/2026-07-06-doss-positioning.md)) — the concern isn't hypothetical; it's the current message. Composability is claimable by Airtable/Retool and eventually incumbents.
- 2026-07-06: NetSuite Next (agentic AI on incumbent architecture) means "modern face + AI chat" will be table stakes ([landscape research](../inputs/research/2026-07-06-ai-erp-landscape.md)).

## Evidence against

- 2026-07-06: Composability *is* winning deals today (Verve, Mezcla, Spread The Love time-savings stories) — rung 1 may be weak as a moat but strong as a wedge ([doss.com research](../inputs/research/2026-07-06-doss-positioning.md)).

## Current synthesis (v1)

STRATEGY.md now frames this as a three-rung ladder: composability (table stakes) → unified operational data / IDP (compounding moat) → adaptivity (structural bet). The open question is no longer *whether* to go beyond tables-and-forms but *how fast rung 3 must become real*.

## How to test

- In every won/lost deal, capture *why* in [inputs/customers/](../inputs/customers/): did differentiation actually matter, and which kind?
- Ask 5 current customers: "If DOSS disappeared tomorrow, what would you go back to and what would you miss most?" The answer locates the real differentiation.
- Competitive teardown: what have NetSuite/Odoo/Fulfil/Cin7 shipped in the last 12 months that narrows any candidate gap?
