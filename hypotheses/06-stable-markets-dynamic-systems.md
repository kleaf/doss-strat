# H6 — The intersection bet: stable demand, accelerating change, adaptive systems

**Status:** gathering evidence
**Opened:** 2026-07-06
**Source:** [Kurt's initial observations](../inputs/notes/2026-07-06-initial-observations.md); ref: [bayeslord on AI capability trajectory](https://x.com/bayeslord/status/2072056960430789032)

## Claim

Two facts hold simultaneously about DOSS's chosen market (physical goods, starting CPG):

1. **Demand-side stability.** People will still buy coffee and detergent in five years. AI improving by orders of magnitude does not disrupt the underlying economy DOSS serves — unlike, say, software tooling markets that AI may consume outright. This makes the market a durable place to build.
2. **Supply-side acceleration.** AI *does* accelerate product innovation within these companies: more SKUs, faster launches, more channels, more experimentation. The operational complexity these businesses must manage goes up, and the rate at which their systems must change goes up with it.

Therefore: the need for an ERP that adapts as fast as the business grows *because of* AI, in a market that is safe *from* AI. DOSS positions at that intersection — the adaptive system ([H5](05-agentic-dynamic-erp.md)) for the stable-but-accelerating economy.

## Why it matters

- This is the macro narrative for investors and for long-horizon roadmap conviction. It answers "why is an AI-native ERP a durable business rather than a feature GPT-7 eats?"
- It sharpens vertical selection: pick verticals where demand is stable but product/channel innovation is accelerating. CPG fits; what's next should be chosen by the same filter.

## Assumptions to check

- Product innovation velocity in mid-market CPG is actually rising (SKU proliferation, channel proliferation) — find data, not vibes.
- Rising innovation velocity actually translates into ERP change pressure (vs. companies just tolerating messier ops).
- The window is real: incumbents can't retrofit adaptivity before we reach scale.

## Evidence for

- 2026-07-06: The macro narrative has independent believers — a16z's "AI is to ERP what cloud was to NetSuite" thesis, and $250M+ deployed into AI-ERP startups in 2025 ([landscape research](../inputs/research/2026-07-06-ai-erp-landscape.md)).

## Evidence against

- 2026-07-06: Incumbent counter-narrative exists ("AI is changing ERP, not replacing it" — Forbes, May 2026). NetSuite Next puts a clock on the window: if agents-on-static-architecture proves good enough, the intersection bet loses its urgency ([landscape research](../inputs/research/2026-07-06-ai-erp-landscape.md)).

## How to test

- Pull industry data on SKU counts / launch cadence / channel mix in mid-market CPG over the last 5 years.
- In customer calls, listen for change-pressure moments: "we added TikTok Shop / a second 3PL / a co-packer and everything broke." Tally frequency in [inputs/calls/](../inputs/calls/).
- Write the investor-grade version of this narrative and stress-test it with 2–3 skeptical readers.
