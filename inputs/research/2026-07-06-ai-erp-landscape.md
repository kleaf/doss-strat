# Next-generation AI-enabled ERP landscape

**Source:** web research 2026-07-06 — a16z enterprise newsletter (Feb 2025), funding coverage, vendor comparisons.

## The cohort

| Company | Funding | Lane | Approach |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Rillet** | $70M B (Aug 2025) | Finance/GL | AI-native ledger, "zero-day close," ex-EY/auditor DNA |
| **Campfire** | $65M B / $100M total (Oct 2025) | Finance/GL | Full ERP replacement for high-growth software cos outgrowing QuickBooks/NetSuite |
| **DualEntry** | — | Finance/GL | AI in every financial workflow; 24-hr "NextDay Migration" from legacy |
| **Nominal** | $20M A (Jul 2025) | Finance layer | Co-pilot on top of existing ERPs; "shadow general ledger" |
| **DOSS** | $73M total | **Operations / value chain** | Composable ops platform for physical-goods mid-market |

**Key structural fact:** nearly the entire AI-ERP cohort is *finance-first* (GL, close, consolidation), mostly for software/tech companies. DOSS is close to alone in the **operations-first, physical-goods** lane.

## a16z thesis (next-gen ERP opportunity)

- AI is to ERP what cloud was for NetSuite's rise. Incumbent ERPs are "historical systems of record," weak at data ingestion and cross-module reconciliation. Mid-market implementations run 4–6 months (often 12+); enterprise 4–6 years with consulting > software cost.
- Three winning paths: **(1) vertical ERPs**, **(2) AI-native layers** ("systems of engagement") atop existing ERPs, **(3) greenfield next-gen ERPs** (names Doss, Rillet, Campfire, Toolkit).
- Strategy guidance: target specific size/complexity bands, not everyone; catch **transition moments** (graduating QuickBooks, underutilized NetSuite contracts); "replacing the ERP requires trust" → **start with a wedge and expand**.

## Incumbent response

- **NetSuite Next** (announced Oct 2025): AI-embedded suite, conversational + agentic workflows.
- SAP S/4HANA embedding Joule copilot (Jan 2025).
- Forbes (May 2026): "AI is changing ERP, not replacing it" — incumbents frame AI as feature, not architecture.

## Signal

- Finance-first crowding validates the category while leaving DOSS's ops lane uncontested → **H2**, **H3**
- a16z "wedge + trust + transition moments" logic matches DOSS's land-beside-QuickBooks stance → **H2**
- NetSuite Next is the incumbent-response clock: bolt-on agents vs. adaptive architecture is the fight → **H1**, **H5**, **H6**
- Layer-vs-replacement is a live strategic choice the cohort splits on; DOSS currently plays both ("middleware" in press, "ERP evolved" on site) → new strategic question
