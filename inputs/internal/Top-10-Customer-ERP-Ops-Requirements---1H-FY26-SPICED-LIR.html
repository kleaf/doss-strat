Refining the outcome, the LIR lens

The needs above are stated as end-states ("know what I have," "trust my margins"). The Value Loops / LIR work sharpens this: churn is throughput failure, not fit failure. Go-live is not the value event, customers churned because "real transactional volume never ran through DOSS automatically." So the outcome a buyer actually retains on is not a feature; it is a recurring transactional cadence with zero manual fallback, made visible by an outcome receipt (a CFO-forwardable digest). And the way to get there is to land narrow: one productized Value Loop, ~6-week target, wiring only the 2 to 3 rails that loop needs, not the whole stack.
The metric we're deploying to reach
LIR, Leading Indicator of Retention: P% of live ICP customers auto-process ≥ 5 transactional documents (orders + POs + invoice/journal syncs) through DOSS every week, with zero manual-fallback events in the trailing 30 days. The activation event that starts the clock: the first end-to-end transaction succeeds and repeats for two consecutive cycles, this replaces the go-live flag. Manual-fallback (keeping/reverting to Sheets + QBO) is the inverse signal and the churn siren.

Method. Population = 77 Salesforce opportunities that entered Stage 2, Discovery or later between Jan 1 and Jun 30, 2026 (1H FY26), replicating the "entered discovery" scope. 66 carried substantive discovery notes; 11 (tests, renewals, placeholders) were excluded. Needs were extracted from each deal's own Identify Pain, Metrics, Metrics L1, Decision Criteria, Scope, Description and Competitors Seen fields, then clustered into themes and ranked by the number of distinct deals citing each. A deal can cite several themes.

SPICED mapping. Each substantive deal was assigned one primary archetype (A, F, or X = Builder / API-first / other) from its own notes; the "primary" shown per requirement is the archetype most represented among the deals citing that need, with the full distribution beside it. Archetype definitions per Who We Sell To, A SPICED Model of the DOSS Buyer and Qualified Pipeline, Entered Discovery Q2 FY26.

LIR lens. The refinement section applies Value Loops, Outcome-Based Productization and the DOSS LIR. Value Loop names, activation events, cadences and tradeoffs (D1, D4) are quoted from that page; loop→archetype pairings follow its five-loop table.
