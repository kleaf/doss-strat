The DOSS Value Stack

Five transactional loops on the module architecture, chained into figure-8s, materialized as living composites, consumed by the customer's named BusinessProcesses. Retention is instrumented at every level against TTRTX, time-to-repeated-transactions: the clock runs until transactions repeat on the business's natural rhythm. The stack is the customer's enterprise hierarchy of needs; DOSS retention deepens as they climb it.

Supersedes loop taxonomy in · Value Loops v2 (/a/28dd2791a8394545) Composes with · L2L instrumentation spec (l2l-app/docs, rev 3) Evidence · 13 install maps · 95 gold cards · churn + objectives corpus
The stack in four moves
  • 1Land the TX loops. Get transactions repeating on the business's natural rhythm (TTRTX). This is survival, and the churn instrument.
  • 2Chain loops into figure-8s. Every seam reconciles on every pass; seam integrity is what Mezcla calls "button-click trust."
  • 3Materialize the composites. Living views the customer's daily questions depend on, integrating 1p + 3p sources continuously.
  • 4Name the BusinessProcesses. DOSS acts; exceptions surface to humans. Autonomous actions carry the highest switching cost.

1 · The architecture and its five TX loops L1 · survival

Every loop lives on exactly one surface of the module architecture, and none of them runs alone: the three operating loops weave through shared seams like a figure-8, while Op/Fin and UMD run tangent to all three. Every operational transaction posts money at a top seam and reads identity at a bottom one.

The metric: TTRTX [PROPOSED]

TTRTX (time-to-repeated-transactions): kickoff → the loop's transaction chain completes in N consecutive cadence periods (default N=3) at the external boundary, zero work-leaving-DOSS events.
TTX (first transaction, any rail) remains the week-1 clock; TTRTX subsumes TTA as the primary clock. The LIR reads repetition: P% of live ICP customers hold every declared loop's cadence each period.

2 · Figure-8s: loops of loops L2 · controls + visibility

A figure-8 chains TX loops through a shared crossing. The lobes are the loops, already cycling on their own; the crossing is the seam where a record must hand off cleanly between them every cycle. That crossing is where the fleet breaks today, which is why the figure-8 is the control: the weave forces the seam to reconcile on every pass.

"Everything business-critical works, but I want button-click trust. Do something and forget it, not monitor three other systems."
Justin, Mezcla, Jan 2026

A customer's stack level is itself an account-health signal.
