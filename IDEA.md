# DOSS strategy

I want to create a framework for tracking the ongoing product strategy of DOSS. I'd like to have as many inputs as possible that you're able to get from various company channels (fireflies, slack, linear, etc.) as well as my own evolving thoughts and questions on what we've built, what customers are asking for, and how we truly add value and differentiation and carve out a powerful growth path to go after the ERP market.

This doesn't need to be an application per se, it can be a set of markdown files although I'd like the ability to have key work product converted into nice typescript presentation page so I can present them in a visually appealing way to larger groups.

Here are my basic hypothesis and questions from my initial observations:

* How do we make this not just "tables and forms" and find true differentation? In other words, what makes this different from what ERP has looked like for the last 30 years, just with a modern face?
* It does seem like there's an interesting angle around operations especially in the mid-market; bringing together data from ordering, web sites, 3pl, quickbooks, etc. to track the entire end-to-end process and optimize the value chain
* I think some degree of focus and emphasis around ERP is critical; we're not doing HR or CRM or finance, we're trying to really do resource planning to optimize how organizations generate revenue and manage costs
* In the calls I've listened to, there doesn't seem to be value positioned really front and center... more like traditional ERP and getting down into the weeds on integration specifics, etc. -- this is important, but what's really the customer pain and the secret sauce that AI enables?
* I think it can enable radically easier implementations, as well as potentially allowing organizations to adapt much more quickly and potentially dynamically evolve their schemas and back end systems to optimize in some way that's abstracted from the end user -- this could be a new agentic form of running an ERP that's fundamentally dynamic instead of being static
* A truism -- AI will improve by several orders of magnitude over the next couple of years (see https://x.com/bayeslord/status/2072056960430789032). But products that DOSS is focused on being the ERP for (starting with consumer packaged goods) will remain relatively stable -- everyone will still need to go and buy coffee and detergent and AI won't disrupt that in the same sort of fundamental way. So that part of the economy will be stable in one sense. But in another sense, product innovation may increase and so the need for ERP to be more dynamic will also improve. Can we be the system at that intersection?
